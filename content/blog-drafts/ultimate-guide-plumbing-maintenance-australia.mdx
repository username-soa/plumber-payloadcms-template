Owning a home in Australia comes with its fair share of maintenance responsibilities, and few systems are as critical—or as potentially expensive—as your plumbing. From the shifting soils of Melbourne causing cracked pipes to the mineral-heavy water of Adelaide affecting hot water systems, our unique environment demands vigilance.

In this comprehensive guide, we'll walk you through everything you need to know to keep your home's waterworks running smoothly, ensuring you stay compliant with strict Australian standards (AS/NZS 3500) while saving thousands in potential repairs.

## The High Cost of Leaks

It’s easy to ignore a dripping tap, but the cumulative effect on your water bill and the environment is staggering. In our drought-prone climate, water efficiency isn't just a trend; it's a necessity.

Limescale buildup inside taps (washers and seats) is the most common culprit. While replacing a washer is a standard fix, persistent leaks might indicate high water pressure, which requires a Pressure Limiting Valve (PLV) installed at the meter.

## Blocked Drains: The Silent Destroyer

Tree roots are the number one cause of blocked sewer lines in Australia. Our native gums and hungry figs can detect moisture from microscopic cracks in old earthenware pipes, invading them and causing catastrophic blockages.

If you notice slow draining sinks or gurgling toilets, you likely have a blockage forming.
